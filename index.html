Who am I?

My name is Udo Guye, and I am a recent graduate of Civil and Environmental Engineering from Addis Ababa University. As a young professional passionate about community development, I have witnessed firsthand the challenges faced by students and families in the Guji Zone of Oromia, Ethiopia.

It is with this motivation that I have founded the "Waldaa Barattoota Godina Gujii" - The Guji Zone Student Association.

Introduction

The Guji Zone Student Association, named “Waldaa Barattoota Godina Gujii,” is an initiative aimed at uplifting the educational standards and addressing the social challenges within the Guji Zone. This proposal outlines the vision, objectives, challenges, and benefits of establishing this association.

Objectives of Guji Zone Student Association

  • Establishing the "Aagaa School".
  • Integrating Governmental Bodies to Find Solutions.

Target Students

The Aagaa School will be open to students who have achieved high scores during the 8th grade examination from the Guji Zone woredas.

Implementation Strategy

  • Community Participation.
  • Awareness Campaigns.

Justification for the Initiative

  • Personal Experience.
  • Potential for Excellence.
  • Increased Engagement and Motivation.
  • Happiness and Well-being.

Securing Initial Funding for the Aagaa School

  • Organizing fundraising sessions.
  • Inspiring the diaspora community.
  • Requesting contributions from Guji Zone administration workers.
  • Soliciting support from influential figures.

Challenges and Threats

  • Funding Limitations.
  • Community Resistance.
  • Sustainability Concerns.
  • Logistical Issues.
  • Economic Instability.

Benefits of the Guji Zone Student Association

  • Enhanced Education Opportunities.
  • Poverty Reduction.
  • Health Improvements.
  • Community Empowerment.
  • Future Leaders.

Leadership of the Association

Selection Criteria

  • Willingness to serve the community.
  • Essential skills.
  • Positive impact on the community.

Selection Process

  • Nomination by community members.
  • Application submission.
  • Evaluation by a selection committee.

Compensation for Association Workers

Fair compensation is crucial for attracting dedicated personnel.

Compensation Structure

  • Full-Time Commitment.
  • Part-Time Roles.

Addressing Sustainability Issues

  • Comprehensive Financial Planning.
  • Building Partnerships.
  • Utilizing Community Resources effectively.

A Vision for a Brighter Future

Imagine a place where every talented student in the Guji Zone has the opportunity to realize their potential.
